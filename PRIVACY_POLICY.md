# Privacy Policy

**Application:** YouTube Video Automation  
**Provider:** Learning By Tutorials  
**Effective date:** May 30, 2026  
**Last updated:** May 30, 2026

## 1. Overview

This Privacy Policy explains how the YouTube Video Automation desktop application (the "Application") handles information when you use its content-creation and publishing features.

The Application is designed to run locally on your computer. It does not require the Application provider to operate a separate server for normal use. However, information is transmitted to third-party services when you choose to use integrations such as OpenAI, YouTube, or TikTok.

## 2. Information the Application Processes

Depending on the features you use, the Application may process:

- topics, titles, paragraphs, templates, prompts, and generated articles;
- slide decks, videos, audio files, thumbnails, captions, descriptions, tags, playlists, categories, languages, recording dates, locations, and publishing schedules;
- locally selected file paths and application settings;
- third-party OAuth authorization tokens and refresh tokens;
- third-party account identifiers and creator information returned by authorized APIs; and
- upload status information and identifiers needed to prevent accidental duplicate uploads.

## 3. How Information Is Used

The Application uses information only to provide requested functionality, including:

- generating articles;
- generating and preparing slide decks;
- generating videos and thumbnails;
- saving user preferences;
- authenticating with authorized third-party services;
- uploading, scheduling, or publishing content;
- reading publishing options supported by a connected account; and
- tracking upload state to support retries and avoid duplicate uploads.

## 4. Local Storage

The Application may store information locally on your computer, including:

- application settings;
- generated articles, slide decks, prompts, videos, thumbnails, and logs;
- OAuth token-cache files;
- upload-state files; and
- paths to files selected by the user.

Local files remain under your control. You are responsible for protecting your computer, backups, API credentials, and token-cache files.

## 5. Third-Party Services

When you use an integration, relevant information is sent to the selected third-party service. These services process information according to their own privacy policies and terms.

Third-party services may include:

- [OpenAI](https://openai.com/policies/privacy-policy/) for article generation;
- [Google](https://policies.google.com/privacy) for Google-related tools and YouTube account authorization;
- [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/howyoutubeworks/user-settings/privacy/) for video uploads and publishing;
- [TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/page/us/privacy-policy/en) for account authorization and video publishing when TikTok integration is enabled; and
- Microsoft PowerPoint and locally installed media-processing tools used on your computer.

## 6. TikTok Data

When TikTok integration is enabled and you connect a TikTok account, the Application may use TikTok OAuth authorization and approved scopes to perform actions that you request.

Depending on the approved scopes and enabled features, the Application may process:

- TikTok access tokens and refresh tokens;
- TikTok account identifiers;
- creator nickname or username;
- available privacy-level options;
- allowed video duration;
- interaction settings for comments, duet, and stitch; and
- uploaded video content, captions, hashtags, cover-frame selections, disclosure settings, and publishing status.

TikTok information is used only to display available publishing options, upload content, publish content with your authorization, and check publishing status.

The Application does not request your TikTok password. TikTok credentials are entered only on TikTok's authorization pages.

## 7. YouTube Data

When you connect a YouTube account, the Application may use Google OAuth authorization and approved scopes to upload videos, set metadata, assign playlists, upload thumbnails, and schedule publication.

The Application does not request your Google password. Google credentials are entered only on Google's authorization pages.

## 8. Data Sharing

The Application does not sell personal information.

The Application sends information to third-party services only when required to perform actions that you initiate or configure. Information may also be disclosed if required by law or necessary to protect legal rights and security.

## 9. Data Retention and Deletion

Generated content, configuration files, logs, token caches, and upload-state files are stored locally until you delete them.

To remove locally stored information:

1. Close the Application.
2. Delete generated output files, logs, settings files, token-cache files, and upload-state files from the Application folder.
3. Revoke third-party authorization through the relevant platform's account settings.

Revoking authorization prevents future API access but does not automatically remove content that you previously published.

## 10. Data Security

The Application is designed to keep local files on your computer unless they are sent to a selected third-party service for processing or publishing.

You should:

- protect token-cache files and API credentials;
- avoid committing secrets or tokens to public source-control repositories;
- use device security controls;
- remove access tokens before sharing logs or configuration files; and
- revoke authorization if you suspect unauthorized access.

No method of storage or transmission is completely secure.

## 11. Children's Privacy

The Application is not directed to children under the age required to independently consent to data processing in their jurisdiction. Do not use the Application to collect or process children's personal information without appropriate authorization.

## 12. Your Choices

You may:

- choose whether to connect a third-party account;
- review requested OAuth permissions before authorizing them;
- decline or revoke third-party access;
- delete local files;
- choose whether to publish generated content; and
- review and edit content and metadata before upload.

## 13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated periodically. The updated version will include a revised "Last updated" date.
